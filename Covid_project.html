COVID-19 Exploration In SQL

This project aimed to conduct a comprehensive analysis of COVID-19 statistics provided by the World Health Organization, focusing on trends in case counts, death rates, and vaccination progress across different regions.

Working with a dataset containing over 300,000 rows required efficient SQL navigation and a strategic approach to data analysis to extract meaningful insights. Utilizing CASE statements allowed me to handle division by zero errors effectively, ensuring the integrity of my calculations for death percentages across various locations. By calculating the death percentage and population infection rates, I was able to uncover trends and correlations that reveal the impact of COVID-19 on different countries and continents.

Key Findings:

  • The analysis revealed that certain continents exhibited higher death counts relative to their populations, highlighting disparities in healthcare systems and responses to the pandemic. Some notiable continents being Europe, Asia, and North America.
  • By examining total cases versus population across different countries, I was able to illustrate the varying degrees of impact COVID-19 has had, shedding light on nations that were more severely affected.
